# Interview Guidelines (Jobscan Methodology)

## tl;dr

- **Prepare 3-5 STAR stories that flex across questions.** Situation, Task, Action, Result. One story should answer 2-3 different behavioral prompts.
- **Keep answers 60-90 seconds.** Research the company (47% of managers reject candidates with no company knowledge). Send a thank-you email within 24 hours. Follow up a second time after 1 week. Stop after 3 unanswered follow-ups.
- **Never discuss salary first. Ask for their range. If forced, give a researched range slightly above target (employers pick the low end).** Employers expect candidates to negotiate — not negotiating can signal poor job fit.

---

## 1. STAR Method — The Core Framework

**S**ituation — Set the scene briefly (where, when, role).
**T**ask — The challenge or responsibility you faced.
**A**ction — What *you* specifically did (not "we").
**R**esult — Measurable outcome (numbers preferred).

Source: https://www.jobscan.co/blog/job-interview-tips/ (tip #4: "Prepare STAR stories")

### Prep Volume
- Jobscan: "prepare three to five STAR stories that cover your biggest wins and toughest challenges"
- One story should flex to answer 2-3 questions (most versatile prep tool)

### Story Selection Rules
- **Choose examples you remember clearly enough to explain thought process AND specific steps.** Interviewers want to hear *why* you did things.
- **Prefer end-to-end stories** — situations you saw through from beginning to completion.
- **Bonus for collaboration** — stories that include working with other people.

Source: https://www.jobscan.co/blog/10-common-behavioral-interview-questions-answer/

## 2. Behavioral Question Categories

Prepare one STAR story for each of these themes. Jobscan's 10-question article centers on these types:

| Category | Typical Prompt | What Interviewer is Assessing |
|---|---|---|
| **Leadership** | "Tell me about a time you led a team" | Ownership, influence, direction-setting |
| **Conflict** | "Tell me about a time you had a conflict with a coworker" | EQ, listening, compromise |
| **Failure** | "Tell me about a time you failed" | Self-awareness, learning from mistakes |
| **Initiative** | "Tell me about a time you went beyond the job description" | Proactivity, ownership |
| **Problem-solving** | "Tell me about a difficult problem you solved" | Analytical thinking, creativity |
| **Pressure / Deadlines** | "Tell me about a time you had to complete a task under a tight deadline" | Time management, prioritization |
| **Teamwork** | "Tell me about a team project" | Collaboration, communication |
| **Adaptability** | "Tell me about a time you had to change approach mid-project" | Flexibility, response to change |
| **Difficult stakeholder** | "Tell me about a time you managed up" | Political / interpersonal skill |
| **Process improvement** | "Tell me about a time you improved a process" | Systems thinking, impact |

### Conflict Stories — Specific Rule
Do NOT describe an ongoing argument or a fight. Describe a situation where you **presented your argument calmly and listened to the other person's position**. Interviewers are looking for emotional intelligence and resolution skill, not wins.

Source: https://www.jobscan.co/blog/10-common-behavioral-interview-questions-answer/

## 3. Common Non-Behavioral Questions

### "Tell Me About Yourself"

Jobscan's **Present-Past-Future formula**:

1. **Present** — Current role + major accomplishments
2. **Past** — How you got here (education, key skills developed)
3. **Future** — What's next and why this role excites you

**Length:** 1-2 minutes. Longer = rambling.

**Avoid:**
- Vague generalities
- Personal details unrelated to the role
- Industry jargon overload
- Memorized robotic delivery
- Failing to connect to the specific job

Source: https://www.jobscan.co/blog/how-to-answer-tell-me-about-yourself-interview-question/

### "What's Your Greatest Weakness?"

**Be genuinely honest.** Don't deflect with "I work too hard."

- Pick a real weakness that is NOT a core job requirement.
- Describe what you're doing about it.
- Show measurable improvement.

Jobscan: "It's natural to want to downplay your weaknesses — don't! Instead, demonstrate self-awareness and commitment to growth."

Source: https://www.jobscan.co/blog/interview-questions/

### "Why Do You Want This Job?"

List the **top 3 appealing things** about the position. Connect your skills to those aspects.

Framework: "The role's day-to-day responsibilities match [X, Y, Z], and my background in [A, B] makes me a strong fit."

### "Why Are You Leaving?"

**Rule:** Never bad-mouth previous employers, bosses, or colleagues.

Frame it as what you **want next** (growth, scope, new challenge), not what you're escaping. Jobscan notes that badmouthing "signals immaturity and negativity."

Source: https://www.jobscan.co/blog/interview-questions/ ; https://www.jobscan.co/blog/job-interview-tips/ (tip #17)

## 4. Interview Prep (Step-by-Step)

Jobscan's 8-step framework:

1. **Dress appropriately** — research the company dress code, dress "a step up"
2. **Research the company + job** — About page, staff bios, news, Glassdoor reviews, customer reviews
3. **Learn about your interviewer** — LinkedIn, Facebook, Instagram (shared interests, educational background) for natural rapport building
4. **Define your professional identity** — strengths, weaknesses (framed positively), what differentiates you
5. **Practice tough interview questions** — Glassdoor for company-specific, mock interviews with a friend
6. **Plan transportation** — add 15-20 minutes buffer, identify parking, backup plans
7. **Self-care** — sleep, healthy meal, hydrate, moderate caffeine
8. **Confirm attendance** — respond promptly, send your own confirmation message

Source: https://www.jobscan.co/how-to-prepare-for-a-job-interview

### Using the Job Description to Prepare
Source: https://www.jobscan.co/blog/preparing-for-an-interview-use-the-job-description/

**For hard skills listed in the posting:**
- Brainstorm one talking point per skill
- Focus on accomplishments, milestones, goals you've achieved using that skill

**For soft skills listed:**
- Create stories demonstrating those characteristics
- Example: if posting emphasizes "highly communicative and client-centric," prepare an example of exceeding normal duties for a client

**For skill gaps:**
- Acknowledge honestly
- Share related competencies that accelerate your learning curve
- Demonstrate commitment to skill development
- **Never exaggerate skills you don't have** — "could even get you fired"

**For questions you'll ask:**
- Identify gaps the posting doesn't fill (culture, structure, team)
- Prepare 5-8 questions in those gaps

## 6. Answer Length & Pacing

- **Target 60-90 seconds per answer.** (Jobscan tip #12)
- Read body language — adjust based on engagement signals.
- Strategic pauses: "Great question; let me think about that for a moment" buys thinking time without filler words.

Source: https://www.jobscan.co/blog/job-interview-tips/

## 7. Questions to Ask the Interviewer

**Prepare 5-8 topics across categories.** Five = variety without running dry. Eight = manageable enough to choose strategically.

Source: https://www.jobscan.co/blog/questions-to-ask-in-an-interview/

### About the Role
- "What does a typical day look like in this role?"
- "How does this role contribute to the organization's broader goals?"
- "What are the biggest challenges someone in this position faces?"

### Performance Metrics
- "What metrics or goals will my performance be evaluated against?"
- "Can you outline the most important milestones for the first 30, 60, and 90 days?"

### Team & Management
- "Can you tell me about the team I'd be working with?"
- "How would you describe your management style?"

### Culture
- "What do new hires typically find surprising after their first few weeks?"

### Growth
- "Have people previously in this role advanced within the organization? Where are they now?"
- "Are there opportunities for stretch assignments where I can build new skills?"

### Closing (always ask one of these)
- "Do you have any hesitations about my background that I can address?" — this one surfaces objections you can handle in real time
- "What are the next steps in the hiring process?"

### Questions to AVOID (until after an offer)
- Salary
- Benefits / PTO / vacation
- Flexible scheduling / remote work (unless role explicitly offers it)
- Basic facts researchable online ("What does your company do?")

## 8. Virtual / Zoom Interviews

Source: https://www.jobscan.co/blog/zoom-interviews-8-tips-for-your-video-interview/ + tips article

1. **Eliminate distractions** — silence notifications, private/quiet room
2. **Curate background** — neutral wall or home-office backdrop (especially for remote roles)
3. **Master lighting** — natural light from a window *facing* you, never behind you ("backlighting never works")
4. **Use headphones** — better audio, less echo
5. **Arrive early** — alarm at 10-minute and 1-minute marks
6. **Don't interrupt** — video lag makes polite interjections sound rude
7. **Dress completely (yes, pants too)** — boosts confidence and prevents awkward standing
8. **Test technology beforehand** — mic, camera, connection, software, with a friend

### Camera & Eye Contact
- Camera at **eye level** (stack books or use a laptop stand).
- Sit at arm's length from the camera.
- **Look at the camera, not the screen,** when speaking — simulates eye contact.

## 9. Phone Interviews

Source: https://www.jobscan.co/blog/phone-interview-tips/

### Physical Setup
- **Stand up** — better posture → better breath → better voice
- **Feet grounded** — reduces nervous energy
- **Hands free** — avoid crossing arms; gestures help words flow
- **Headset, not holding the phone** — frees hands, improves posture

### Verbal Pacing
- Don't rush to fill silence. Use "Let me think about that" as a pause filler.
- Have prepared stories + notes in front of you — you can reference them without being seen.

### Materials to Have
- Research notes about the company
- Pre-written STAR stories covering key competencies
- List of questions you plan to ask
- Your own resume
- A glass of water

## 10. Salary Negotiation

Source: https://www.jobscan.co/blog/how-to-negotiate-salary/ ; https://www.jobscan.co/blog/answer-salary-expectations/

### When to Negotiate
- **Never on the resume.**
- **Never during the phone screen** (unless they ask).
- **Never after accepting.**
- **Only during the offer / negotiation stage**, typically on a phone/video call with the hiring manager or recruiter.

Career advisor Bob McIntosh (via Jobscan): "Never be the first one to bring it up. Talking about benefits can also be construed as inappropriate."

### Research First
Before any salary conversation:
- **PayScale, Salary.com, Glassdoor** — published ranges
- **LinkedIn / Levels.fyi / Indeed / Robert Half salary guide** — role-specific benchmarks
- **Talk to someone inside the company** if possible — "the best way to determine what an employer can afford"

### When They Ask "What Are Your Salary Expectations?"

Four Jobscan-endorsed strategies:

**1. Deflect (preferred)**
> "I would prefer to know more about the job and your expectations of the employee first to give you a realistic number."
Be ready for follow-up pressure — they'll often push.

**2. Give a range**
> "I'm looking for a position which pays between $120,000 and $140,000 for the level of responsibility."
Inflate slightly — **employers pick the low end** of your range. If your real target is $130K, ask for $135-$155K.

**3. Range + negotiable benefits**
> "I'm looking at $120-140K, but that number can be adjusted based on remote work, vacation time, bonus structure, and equity."

**4. Turn it around**
> "What budget has the company set for this role?"
Sometimes they'll tell you. If they do, you've won an information asymmetry.

### Key Mistakes to Avoid

- **Don't undersell yourself** to win the job. They'll pay the lowball.
- **Don't accept the first offer** just to avoid seeming greedy. Employers expect negotiation; not negotiating can signal poor self-advocacy.
- **Don't ask for double the offer** or tens of thousands above market — research-backed counters only.

### If Uncomfortable Mid-Negotiation
> "Can I have some time to think about this offer?"
Always acceptable. Gives you time to research or talk to a mentor.

### Walk-Away Number
Know yours *before* the conversation starts. This is the number below which you decline — and you must be willing to decline.

## 11. Thank-You Emails

Source: https://www.jobscan.co/blog/thank-you-email-interview/ ; https://www.jobscan.co/blog/how-to-follow-up-after-a-job-interview/

### Timing
- **Send within 24 hours.**
- **Optimal window:** lunchtime the next day.
- **Friday interview:** same evening OR Monday lunchtime (NOT the weekend).
- Early-in-the-day interview → same business day if possible.

### Structure
1. Personalized greeting with interviewer's name
2. Brief thanks for their time
3. **Specific reference to something discussed** — shows active listening
4. Reaffirm interest in the role
5. Include any promised materials or availability info

### Length
**Short and sweet.** 3-5 sentences. Don't overwhelm.

### Critical Rules
- **Send separate emails per interviewer** — NEVER one generic blast to multiple recipients.
- Customize the "specific reference" per recipient.
- Double-check names, titles, and company spelling.

### Template
> Hi [Name],
>
> Thank you for taking the time to meet with me yesterday. I especially enjoyed hearing about [specific topic from the interview]. I'm more excited about the [Role] opportunity than when we started — my experience with [relevant skill/project] would translate directly.
>
> Thank you again for your time. I look forward to hearing about the next steps.
>
> Best regards,
> [Your name]

## 12. Follow-Up Cadence After Interview

Source: https://www.jobscan.co/blog/how-to-follow-up-after-a-job-interview/

| Attempt | Timing | Content |
|---|---|---|
| **1st (thank-you)** | Within 24 hours of interview | See section 11 |
| **2nd follow-up** | 1-2 days after interviewer's stated deadline passes | Polite status check, reiterate interest |
| **3rd follow-up** | 1 week after 2nd | Final status check |
| **Stop** | After 3 unanswered attempts | Redirect energy elsewhere |

### Tone Throughout
- Gracious about their timeline
- Persistent but not aggressive
- **Never** "checking in because I have another offer" unless actually true (burns trust if it isn't)
- Remain professional regardless of disappointment

## 13. Application Follow-Up (Pre-Interview)

Source: https://www.jobscan.co/blog/how-to-follow-up-on-a-job-application/

### When to Reach Out
- **48-72 hours after submission:** initial follow-up expressing interest
- **Through a connection:** follow up within 1 week
- **Through unknown recruiter/manager:** wait 2 weeks
- **Subsequent attempts:** space about 1 week apart
- **Cap at 2 total follow-ups** (initial + one reminder)

### How
- **Email is the "most professional option"** — lets them respond on their time
- Avoid unscheduled phone calls ("really caught off guard" per recruiter quoted by Jobscan)

### What to Include
- Clear subject line: position + your name
- Address the recruiter by name
- Reintroduce + reference the specific role
- Why you're genuinely interested in this company (not just any company)
- Polite status request
- Offer to send additional materials
- Contact info
- Error-free, professional

## 14. In-Person Interview Logistics

Source: https://www.jobscan.co/blog/job-interview-tips/

- **Arrive 10-15 minutes early** — early enough for paperwork / settling, not so early you loiter awkwardly
- **Dress one step above the company's dress code** — err on overdressed
- **Bring:**
  - 2-3 copies of resume (one per interviewer + extra)
  - References list (separate document)
  - Pen and notebook
  - Portfolio if role-appropriate
- **Phone: OFF, not silenced.** 90% of interviewers disqualify for phone-touching.

## 15. Behavioral Pitfalls (Red Flags from the Candidate Side)

Signals that hurt you:

- **Lack of eye contact** (65% rejection rate)
- **No smiling** (40% rejection rate)
- **Arrogance** (76% rejection rate) — overclaiming, dismissing others' contributions
- **Touching phone during interview** (90% disqualification)
- **Badmouthing previous employers**
- **Rambling past 90 seconds** per answer
- **Vague answers with no numbers or specifics**
- **Asking about salary/PTO before receiving an offer**
- **Unprepared for common questions** (shows no effort)
- **No questions for the interviewer** ("no curiosity")
- **Showing up late** without a heads-up

## 16. Prompt-Style Notes for LLM-Driven Interview Coaching

For an LLM helping a candidate prep:

- **Generate 3-5 STAR skeletons** from the candidate's resume that cover: leadership, conflict, failure, pressure, teamwork, initiative.
- **For each STAR story**, force a quantified Result line — refuse outputs without a number/percent/dollar.
- **Ban "we" in the Action step** — candidate must own their role; "I" required.
- **Target 60-90 seconds of spoken output** (≈ 150-220 words per STAR).
- **Reject stories where the candidate blames someone else** — signals poor accountability.
- **Flag when the same story is being reused for >3 different themes** — it's probably not flexing that well.

## 17. Final Interview Checklist

Pre-interview:

- [ ] Company research done (product, news, leadership, Glassdoor)
- [ ] Interviewer research done (LinkedIn background + shared interests)
- [ ] 3-5 STAR stories prepped, each with a quantified result
- [ ] 5-8 questions prepared across role / team / growth / culture / closing
- [ ] Job description mapped — one talking point per required skill
- [ ] Outfit ready, one step above company dress code
- [ ] Logistics planned (route + 15-20 min buffer, backup plan)
- [ ] Phone set to OFF (not silent) before walking in
- [ ] Copies of resume + references + pen + notebook
- [ ] 15-minute pre-interview routine: box breathing, power pose, vocal warmup

Post-interview:

- [ ] Thank-you email within 24 hours, separate to each interviewer, with specific reference
- [ ] Calendar reminder for 2nd follow-up (1-2 days after stated deadline)
- [ ] Stop at 3 unanswered follow-ups
