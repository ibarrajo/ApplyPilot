# Cover Letter Guidelines (Jobscan Methodology)

## tl;dr

- **One page, 250-400 words, 3-4 paragraphs, single-spaced.** Hiring managers won't read longer.
- **Structure as Problem → Solution → Evidence → Call to Action** — not a resume recap. Paragraph 1 states the company's problem (1-2 sentences), paragraph 2 is your solution (1-2 sentences), paragraph 3 is 3-4 sentences of supporting experience, paragraph 4 is a specific next-step CTA (not a generic "thanks").
- **Cover letters with job-description keywords + specific company research lift interview rates 3.4x.** Over 80% of hiring managers read them; 60% of applications require one. Skip "To Whom It May Concern" — it's dead.

---

## 1. Length

### Word Count
**250-400 words total.** Half a page to one full page.

Source: https://www.jobscan.co/cover-letter-writing-guide

### Page Count
**One page is the default standard.** Jobscan: "The traditional one-page cover letter is seen as the best choice in job applications."

Source: https://www.jobscan.co/blog/can-a-cover-letter-be-two-pages/

### Two Pages — When Acceptable
Only under these conditions:
- **10+ years experience** with multiple relevant accomplishments to showcase
- **Management / leadership roles** requiring detailed team/project context
- Job posting explicitly asks for detailed project explanations

**Critical caveat:** "A two-page cover letter should always provide new information. It should not just repeat what is in your resume."

Default to one page unless you can justify every word of a second page.

## 2. Structure — The 4-Paragraph Framework

Jobscan's explicit framework:

### Paragraph 1: The Problem (1-2 sentences)
State the problem the company or role is trying to solve. Shows you've researched the posting and the company. Not a generic intro about yourself.

**Bad opening:** "I am writing to apply for the Senior Engineer position I saw on LinkedIn."

**Good opening:** "With your expansion into real-time fraud detection, your team faces a 10x growth in event throughput this quarter — exactly the problem I spent the past three years solving at [prior company]."

### Paragraph 2: Your Solution (1-2 sentences)
You are the answer to their need. Connect your skills/background directly to the problem from paragraph 1.

### Paragraph 3: Supporting Experience (3-4 sentences)
Evidence: specific accomplishments, metrics, and skills from the job description. This is where keyword integration happens naturally. Tie 2-3 concrete results to what the company needs.

### Paragraph 4: Call to Action (1-2 sentences)
Suggest a next step. NOT "thanks for your consideration."

**Bad close:** "Thank you for your time and consideration. I look forward to hearing from you."

**Good close:** "I'd welcome the chance to discuss how I'd approach the first 90 days of the role. Please let me know your availability next week."

Source: https://www.jobscan.co/blog/how-to-write-a-cover-letter/

## 3. Tone & Voice

Source: https://www.jobscan.co/blog/how-to-write-a-cover-letter/

- **Short words in short sentences.** Reader scans it in < 60 seconds.
- **Professional but not stiff.** Contractions OK (`I'm`, `I've`). Industry jargon moderated.
- **Enthusiasm without desperation.** Show you want this specific role, not any role.
- **Personality OK in moderation.** A brief relevant anecdote is fine; a long joke is not.
- **Address the employer's needs, not your own career desires.**

**Do not say:**
- "I am a hard worker" (cliché, unprovable)
- "I am passionate about..." (overused; show it with evidence)
- "I would be an excellent fit" (subjective; let the evidence argue it)
- "My greatest weakness is that I work too hard"

---

## 4. Greeting & Closing

### Greeting

**Priority order:**

1. **Specific hiring manager's name:** `Dear Ms. Chen,` — find via LinkedIn, company About page, or recruiter.
2. **Team lead title:** `Dear Hiring Manager,` — default if no name found.
3. **Never:** `To Whom It May Concern` — Jobscan calls this "outdated."

### Sign-off
- `Best regards,` or `Kind regards,` followed by your full name.
- Avoid `Sincerely` (dated, but not wrong), `Cheers` (too casual for most industries), `Yours faithfully` (British formal, unusual in US).

Source: https://www.jobscan.co/blog/how-to-write-a-cover-letter/

## 5. Formatting Rules

### Fonts
- Clear fonts: **Arial** or **Times New Roman** (same approved list as resume)
- **Font size: 10-12 pt** (12 pt preferred for readability per spacing article)

### Spacing
- **Single-spaced.** Jobscan: "Single spacing with clear breaks between paragraphs is the best way to format cover letters today."
- **Double spacing is dead.** "The times of using double-spaced cover letters are gone... it's seen as old-fashioned and makes the letter longer than needed."
- **Blank line between paragraphs.**

Source: https://www.jobscan.co/blog/cover-letter-spacing/

### Margins
- **1 inch on all sides.**

Source: https://www.jobscan.co/blog/what-should-a-cover-letter-look-like/

### Business Letter Header
Standard business letter format:
```
[Your Name]
[Your City, State, Zip]
[Your Phone]
[Your Email]

[Date]

[Hiring Manager Name]
[Their Title]
[Company Name]
[Company City, State]

Dear [Name],

[Body]

Best regards,
[Your Name]
```

### File Format
Same rule as resume: **PDF by default, DOCX only if requested.** (Jobscan doesn't give a separate rule for cover letters — inherits from resume guidance.)

## 6. Customization Tactics

### Research Before Writing
1. Check the **company website** — product, recent launches, mission
2. Read **recent news / press releases** — funding rounds, leadership changes
3. Scan **LinkedIn** — growth direction, team size, hiring patterns
4. Review **job description** for specific problems the role exists to solve

### Tailor Every Letter
- Rewrite the opening problem statement per company
- Swap achievement examples to match the target role's weight on each skill
- Change the keyword emphasis to mirror the posting

### What "Customization" Is Not
- Find-and-replace the company name on a template (recruiters spot this immediately)
- Mentioning the company name 6 times to fake personalization

## 7. Keyword Integration

Source: https://www.jobscan.co/cover-letter-writing-guide

**Rule:** keywords belong inside your accomplishment sentences, not as a skills dump.

Cover letters are often parsed into the ATS database alongside the resume. Recruiters search that database by keyword. If the cover letter has `kafka` and `terraform` that the resume doesn't, it can still help you match.

**Do:** Weave keywords naturally into evidence.
> "Led migration from a monolithic Rails stack to a Kafka-backed microservices architecture in 7 months, cutting p99 latency 62%."

**Don't:** Stuff them in a list.
> "I am proficient in Kafka, Terraform, Docker, Kubernetes, AWS, GCP, Python, and Go."

## 8. What to Include vs Avoid

### Include
- **Measurable accomplishments with numbers** (dollars, percents, users, time saved)
- **Skills that match the job description** — exact wording
- **Problem-solving example** specific to the role
- **Industry knowledge** (acronyms, domain terms) demonstrating you belong
- **Teamwork / collaboration** evidence (most roles want this)

### Avoid
- **Repeating the resume verbatim** — waste of the letter
- **Clichés:** "hardworking," "passionate," "team player," "results-driven"
- **Excuses for gaps or firings** — address briefly if unavoidable, don't dwell
- **Irrelevant personal detail** — hobbies unrelated to the role
- **Overly formal distance** — "It would be my honor to be considered..."
- **Negativity** about current or previous employers
- **Salary demands** — save for later in the process

Source: https://www.jobscan.co/blog/how-to-write-a-cover-letter/

## 10. Quality Gates — Things That Get a Cover Letter Read (vs Ignored)

### Read
- Opens with a company-specific hook
- First sentence mentions a real company challenge, not a generic greeting
- Specific metrics in paragraph 3
- CTA proposes a concrete next step (a meeting, a discussion, an availability window)

### Ignored
- Opens with "I am writing to apply for..."
- Reads like a resume translated into prose
- Generic company description that could apply to any competitor
- Closes with "Thank you for your consideration"
- Longer than one page without justification
- Any typos or wrong company name

## 11. LLM Prompt Notes

For generating cover letters programmatically:

- **Hard token budget:** target 280-380 words output (≈ Jobscan's 250-400 range, leave buffer for trim).
- **Prompt the model** to fill a 4-slot template: problem, solution, evidence, CTA.
- **Feed the model the job description + company background** as separate inputs — don't let it guess the company's problem.
- **Ban cliché phrases** in the system prompt: "hard worker", "passionate about", "team player", "go-getter", "results-driven", "proven track record" (vague, universally overused).
- **Require a metric** in at least 2 of the evidence sentences.
- **Ban "To Whom It May Concern"** — default to "Dear Hiring Manager" if no name available.
- **Force a non-generic CTA** — reject outputs that end in "thank you for your consideration" without a concrete proposed next step.

## 12. Final Cover Letter Checklist

Before shipping:

- [ ] 250-400 words, one page, 3-4 paragraphs
- [ ] Opens with the company's specific problem, not a self-introduction
- [ ] Paragraph 2 solution is directly tied to paragraph 1 problem
- [ ] Paragraph 3 has 2+ quantified accomplishments
- [ ] Paragraph 4 proposes a concrete next step (meeting, call, availability)
- [ ] Greeting uses a real name or "Dear Hiring Manager" — not "To Whom It May Concern"
- [ ] Single-spaced, blank line between paragraphs
- [ ] Arial or Times New Roman, 10-12 pt
- [ ] 1-inch margins
- [ ] Keywords from the posting appear naturally in paragraph 3
- [ ] No clichés: "hard worker", "passionate about", "team player", "results-driven"
- [ ] Company name spelled correctly
- [ ] No overlap with resume content — letter adds context, doesn't repeat
- [ ] File format matches resume (PDF default)
- [ ] Filename: `FirstName_LastName_CoverLetter.pdf`
